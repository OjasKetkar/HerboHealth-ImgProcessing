Carom seeds are the seeds of the ajwain herb.Although referred to as “seeds,” carom seeds are the fruit of the ajwain herb.
Ajwain originated in the Middle East, possibly in Egypt. It is now primarily grown and used in the Indian Subcontinent, but also in Iran, Egypt and Afghanistan. It is sometimes used as an ingredient in berbere, a spice mixture favored in Eritrea and Ethiopia

How to grow:
    Method 1– Stem Cutting
  1. Snip off 5-6 inches long tip or branch from a mature plant
  2. Dip the cutting into a rooting hormone and plant in the garden soil or container a few inches deep
  3. ) Place it at a shaded location and keep the soil moist by watering thoroughly
    4. Method 2 – Sowing the Seeds
  4. Sow the seeds few inches deep in the garden with well-amended soil or in the container with a potting mix and mist regularly
  5. Keep the container in partial shade
  6. The seeds will germinate within 7-10 days
